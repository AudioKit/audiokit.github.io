Overview

Physical model related to the striking of a metal block. Audio output is a tone related to the striking of a metal block as found in a vibraphone. The method is a physical model developed from Perry Cook, but re-coded for Csound.

initWithFrequency:amplitude:stickHardness:strikePosition:tremoloShape:tremoloFrequency:tremoloAmplitude:

Instantiates the vibes with all values
{% highlight objective-c %}- (instancetype)initWithFrequency:(AKParameter *)frequency amplitude:(AKParameter *)amplitude stickHardness:(AKConstant *)stickHardness strikePosition:(AKConstant *)strikePosition tremoloShape:(AKTable *)tremoloShape tremoloFrequency:(AKParameter *)tremoloFrequency tremoloAmplitude:(AKParameter *)tremoloAmplitude{% endhighlight %}
  • frequency: Frequency of note played. Updated at Control-rate. [Default Value: 440]
  • amplitude: Amplitude of note. Updated at Control-rate. [Default Value: 1.0]
  • stickHardness: The hardness of the stick used in the strike. A range of 0 to 1 is used. 0.5 is a suitable value. [Default Value: 0.5]
  • strikePosition: Where the block is hit, in the range 0 to 1. [Default Value: 0.2]
  • tremoloShape: Shape of tremolo, usually a sine table, created by a function [Default Value: sine]
  • tremoloFrequency: Frequency of tremolo in Hertz. Suggested range is 0 to 12 Updated at Control-rate. [Default Value: 0]
  • tremoloAmplitude: Amplitude of the tremolo Updated at Control-rate. [Default Value: 0]
