In order to help you find a particular type of operation, they are categorized into pages on this web site as well as folders inside of AudioKit.

CategoryDescription
Analysis Tracking classes that determine the amplitude and frequency of signals.
FFT Spectral functions including cross synthesis, mixing, scaling, warping, and resynthesis.
Mathematical Operations Basic operations such as scaling, mixing, summing, and multiplication of signals.
Signal Generators Classes for physical models, oscillators, linear controls, granular synthesis textures.
Signal Input and Output Classes for handling incoming audio from devices and files as well as outputting audio to a speaker or files.
Signal Modifiers Effects such as convolution, delay, compression, flange, vibrato, reverb, panning, and many filters.

FFT

Spectral functions including cross synthesis, mixing, scaling, warping, and resynthesis.

ClassDescription
AKCrossSynthesizedFFT Performs cross-synthesis between two source F-Signals.
AKFFT Generate an f-Signal from a mono audio source using phase vocoder overlap-add synthesis.
AKFFTProcessor Phase vocoder analysis processing with onset detection/processing.
AKMixedFFT Mix 'seamlessly' two pv signals. This opcode combines the most prominent
AKPhaseLockedVocoder Phase-locked vocoder processing.
AKResynthesizedAudio Resynthesise phase vocoder data (f-signal) using a FFT overlap-add.
AKScaledFFT Scale the frequency components of a pv stream, resulting in pitch shift.
AKSpectralVocoder Combine the spectral envelope of one F-signal with the excitation (frequencies) of another.
AKWarpedFFT Warp the spectral envelope of a PVS signal by means of shifting and scaling.
